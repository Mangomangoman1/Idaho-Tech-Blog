Coverage desk

Six systems to track first.

The Idaho Review starts where technology meets operating constraints: chips, energy, water, broadband, small-business tools, repair, and the workforce that has to make all of it work.

Sector brief

AI & Automation

Applied AI in Idaho is likely to show up first in ordinary workflows: quoting, scheduling, fraud detection, bookkeeping, customer support, field service, irrigation decisions, food processing, and local government paperwork.

What to watch: Watch whether local firms adopt national tools safely, whether small businesses understand data risk, and whether Idaho vendors build industry-specific automation instead of generic chatbot wrappers.

Sector brief

Semiconductors

Micron makes semiconductors a local story. The useful questions are not only chip output, but power, water, land use, suppliers, housing, technical training, and technician pipelines around Boise.

What to watch: Watch Boise fab timelines, CHIPS Act milestones, supplier opportunities, and whether education programs can produce enough workers for memory manufacturing and support roles.

Sector brief

Energy & Nuclear

Eastern Idaho gives the state a national energy role through Idaho National Laboratory, advanced nuclear research, grid security, materials work, and federal energy programs.

What to watch: Watch which projects move from announcement to deployment, how workforce demand changes around Idaho Falls, and how power demand from industry and data centers stresses utility planning.

Sector brief

Agtech & Water

In Idaho, agriculture technology cannot be separated from water. Irrigation sensors, pivots, groundwater models, dairy automation, food processing, and repair access all sit inside water and labor constraints.

What to watch: Watch aquifer management, irrigation modernization, farm-data ownership, equipment repair access, and whether technology lowers risk or just adds another bill.

Sector brief

Broadband & Infrastructure

Idaho broadband is a geography story: mountain valleys, rural roads, tribal lands, fast-growing suburbs, and communities where a grant announcement does not guarantee usable service.

What to watch: Watch BEAD-funded buildouts, affordability, mapping accuracy, reliability, fiber versus fixed wireless, and what happens after the ribbon cutting.

Sector brief

Repair & Hardware

Idaho technology coverage should include breakdowns: farm equipment, routers, laptops, pumps, school devices, business hardware, diagnostics, surplus electronics, and the people who keep systems working.

What to watch: Watch right-to-repair policy, parts delays, diagnostic access, electronics reuse, and the economics of independent repair outside the largest cities.

Interview series

Idaho Builders: five questions with people building useful things.

A lightweight interview format for founders, operators, technicians, engineers, educators, students, researchers, and small-business owners using technology in Idaho.

Suggest an interviewContribute instead

The format

Each interview is short, practical, and edited for clarity. The goal is not celebrity coverage. It is to document what Idaho people are building, adopting, repairing, teaching, funding, and learning.

Five questions

  1. What are you building or working on?
  2. Why does it matter in Idaho?
  3. What part is harder here than outsiders expect?
  4. What changed in the last year?
  5. Who else should Idaho be paying attention to?

Who we want

  • Founders and operators
  • Technical workers and repair people
  • Workforce educators and students
  • Ag, energy, broadband, and public-sector builders
  • Small businesses using technology practically

Outreach script

Hey [Name] — I’m building The Idaho Review, a new publication and company map for Idaho’s technology economy. I’m starting with short 5-question interviews with Idaho founders, operators, educators, technicians, and builders. Would you be open to answering a few questions about what you’re building and what you’re seeing in Idaho tech? The format is lightweight, and I’ll send you the final version before anything goes live.

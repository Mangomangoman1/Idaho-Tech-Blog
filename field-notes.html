First call

A call for Idaho field notes.

The Idaho Review is collecting short, practical notes from people close to Idaho technology: what changed, what worked, what failed, and what outsiders misunderstand.

Share a field noteSend a tip

Technology in Idaho is not only a story about announcements, funding, and public reports. It is also a story about people using tools under real constraints: distance, power, water, hiring, repair, seasons, customers, compliance, and small teams.

We want short field notes from people who are close to that work. You do not need a polished essay. A useful note can be 150 words about a workflow change, a failed automation, a hiring problem, a broadband issue, an equipment repair constraint, a classroom lesson, a farm technology experiment, or a small business using AI carefully.

What to send

  • What happened?
  • Where in Idaho did it happen?
  • Why does it matter?
  • What should other Idaho builders learn from it?

The goal is to build a more accurate map of Idaho’s technology economy from the ground level.
